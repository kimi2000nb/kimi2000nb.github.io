**If your machine displays `Error! [10000001] info(00000000)`, the main shaft is unable to return to its home position (100°). In most cases, the problem is caused by a mechanical jam rather than an electronic failure.**

## Symptoms

You may notice one or more of the following:

- The machine stops during startup.

- The main shaft cannot return to 100°.

- The handwheel is difficult or impossible to turn.

- The machine displays:

## Common Causes

The most common causes include:

- Thread or lint jammed inside the bobbin area.
- The bobbin case is installed incorrectly.
- A broken needle is stuck in the hook.
- Thread wrapped around the rotary hook.
- The machine was powered off during embroidery, causing the shaft position to become incorrect.

## Step 1 — Turn Off the Machine

Before performing any inspection:

- Turn off the machine.
- Disconnect the power.
- Remove the embroidery hoop if installed.

## Step 2 — Inspect the Bobbin Area

Remove the bobbin and bobbin case.

Check for:

- Tangled thread
- Fabric debris
- Broken needle fragments
- Lint buildup

Clean the hook thoroughly before reinstalling the bobbin case.

> **Tip:** Simply adding oil will not solve a mechanical jam. Always clean the hook area first.

## Step 3 — Check the Main Shaft

Slowly rotate the handwheel by hand.

- It should rotate smoothly.
- If it feels tight or becomes stuck, do not force it.
- Reinspect the hook area for anything blocking movement.

## Step 4 — Restart the Machine

After cleaning:

1. Reinstall the bobbin case correctly.
2. Turn the machine back on.
3. Allow the machine to perform its automatic initialization.

If the shaft returns to **100°**, the error should disappear.

## If the Error Remains

If the machine still reports:

```text
Error!
[10000001]
info(00000000)
```

Check for:

- Damaged rotary hook
- Bent needle bar
- Timing issues
- Main shaft obstruction
- Sensor or encoder problems

These issues may require professional inspection.

## Preventive Maintenance

To reduce the chance of this error:

- Clean the hook area every day.
- Remove thread buildup after each project.
- Replace bent or damaged needles immediately.
- Oil the machine according to the maintenance schedule.
- Never force the handwheel when resistance is present.

## Need More Help?

If the problem continues after cleaning the hook and reinstalling the bobbin case, record a short video showing:

- The error screen
- The bobbin area
