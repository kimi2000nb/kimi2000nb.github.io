# How to Adjust Belt Tension

The belt system plays an important role in controlling the movement of the embroidery frame and machine components.

Over time, belts may become loose due to long-term operation, temperature changes, or normal wear. Incorrect belt tension can affect embroidery accuracy and machine performance.

## When Should You Adjust Belt Tension?

Belt tension adjustment may be needed when you notice:

- **Abnormal noise during machine operation**  
  Loose or improperly tensioned belts may create unusual sounds while the machine is running.

- **Embroidery design position shifting**  
  Incorrect belt tension can cause the hoop movement to become inaccurate, resulting in design misalignment.

- **Visible belt looseness**  
  A belt that has noticeable slack may require adjustment.

- **Long-term machine use maintenance**  
  Periodic belt inspection helps maintain stable machine performance.

## Symptoms of Incorrect Belt Tension

### Belt Too Loose

A loose belt may cause:

- Embroidery pattern displacement
- Poor positioning accuracy
- Delayed or uneven movement
- Increased operating noise

### Belt Too Tight

An overly tight belt may cause:

- Increased motor load
- Abnormal mechanical noise
- Faster belt wear
- Reduced machine movement smoothness

Proper belt tension allows the machine to move smoothly while maintaining accurate embroidery positioning.

## How to Adjust Belt Tension

Follow these steps to check and adjust belt tension:

### Step 1: Turn Off the Machine

1. Turn off the embroidery machine.
2. Disconnect the power supply.
3. Move the embroidery arm or hoop carefully to access the belt area.

### Step 2: Check the Belt Condition

Inspect the belt:

- Check for visible looseness.
- Check whether the belt has excessive vibration during operation.
- Check for signs of wear or damage.

### Step 3: Adjust Belt Tension

1. Locate the belt tension adjustment mechanism.
2. Loosen the fixing screws slightly.
3. Adjust the tension until the belt has proper resistance.
4. Tighten the screws securely after adjustment.

Do not over-tighten the belt. The belt should be firm but still allow smooth movement.

### Step 4: Test the Machine

After adjustment:

1. Turn on the machine.
2. Run a test movement or embroidery design.
3. Check:
   - Movement noise
   - Hoop positioning accuracy
   - Embroidery alignment

Fine adjustments may be required depending on machine usage and belt condition.

## Maintenance Tips

- Check belt tension regularly as part of machine maintenance.
- Avoid adjusting belt tension without confirming the actual cause of the issue.
- If the belt is damaged, replace it instead of only adjusting tension.
- After adjustment, always test embroidery accuracy.
