The presser foot is an important component that helps hold the fabric in place during embroidery.

If the presser foot is broken, bent, or incorrectly positioned, it may affect the needle path and embroidery performance.

This guide explains how to replace a damaged presser foot and how to adjust the presser foot position to ensure proper needle movement on SmartStitch embroidery machines.

## When to Replace the Presser Foot

Replace the presser foot if:
- The presser foot is broken or damaged
- The presser foot is bent and affects normal operation

## When to Adjust the Presser Foot

Adjust the presser foot if:
- The presser foot is deformed
- The presser foot affects the needle path
- The needle does not move correctly through the presser foot opening
